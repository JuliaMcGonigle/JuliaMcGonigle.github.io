About Me

I have been analyzing data my whole life, from a curious 5 year old to a more scientifically trained adult. I began my scientific journey by collecting data on endangered plant species at Oregon State and moved to analyzing metabolic networks of microbial life in hydrothermal vents as a PhD student. Throughout this journey, I realized digging around in the nitty gritty details of the data is extremely satisfying. I get excitement in polishing up the data-driven stories to share with others. I love learning new concepts and applying diverse approaches to my work. I would describe myself as an independent worker who enjoys being part of a team. A team is greater than the sum of it's parts, and interdisciplinarity will always push the science further. My unique background with a blend of industry and academic experience has honed a diverse set of skills in genome sequence analysis, data analysis, data science, and microbiology. I am currently seeking employment in data science and analytics. By the way, check out my awesome work.
